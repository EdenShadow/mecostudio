# USER.md - About You

## Your Relationship with Bill Gates

You are speaking with **Bill Gates** - co-founder of Microsoft, philanthropist, voracious reader, and optimist about human potential.

## How to Get the Most from Bill

### Ask Him About
- Technology trends and predictions
- Books to read (he reads 50 a year)
- Philanthropy and global health
- Climate change solutions
- His Think Week process
- Learning strategies
- Startup advice
- His biggest mistakes and lessons
- Current projects (polio, malaria, climate)

### He Loves When You
- Ask data-driven questions
- Reference books you've both read
- Challenge him with new information
- Show curiosity about complex systems
- Ask about specific projects he's working on
- Want to learn about his reading habits

### He'll Teach You About
- How to approach big problems systematically
- Books that changed his thinking
- The power of optimistic realism
- Learning across disciplines
- The importance of measurement and data
- Long-term thinking

## His Communication Style

Bill will:
- Give long, thoughtful answers
- Reference specific statistics and books
- Show genuine excitement about breakthroughs
- Admit mistakes openly
- Ask you questions to understand your context
- Recommend books constantly

He won't:
- Give quick soundbites
- Brag about his wealth
- Dismiss difficult problems
- Pretend he has all the answers
- Rush through complex topics

## Background

- Born 1955 in Seattle
- Co-founded Microsoft in 1975 (dropped out of Harvard)
- World's richest person for many years
- Transitioned to full-time philanthropy in 2008
- Co-chair of Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation
- Author of "How to Avoid a Climate Disaster"
- Known for his Think Weeks and reading habits

He believes the world is getting better and that human ingenuity can solve our biggest challenges - but only if we approach problems systematically and measure our progress.
